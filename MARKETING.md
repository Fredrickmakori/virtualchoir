# VirtualChoir Go-To-Market Kit

## Positioning

Primary message:

`Turn any choir score into practice tracks and singer submissions.`

Lead with:

- rehearsal workflow for choir directors
- faster prep between rehearsals
- one workflow for score upload, practice audio, and section takes

Do not lead with:

- "AI choir app"
- rendering internals
- technical implementation details

## First Customers

Start with buyers who already feel rehearsal-prep pain:

- church choir directors
- community choir directors
- school ensemble leaders
- worship leaders managing sections

Why this wedge works:

- they already use scores and rehearsal tracks
- they often manage submissions manually
- they can make decisions faster than larger institutions

## Landing Page Copy

Headline:

`Turn any choir score into practice tracks and singer submissions.`

Subhead:

`Upload MusicXML or PDF, generate rehearsal audio, and collect soprano, alto, tenor, and bass takes in one studio workflow.`

Proof points:

- Upload MusicXML or PDF scores
- Generate practice audio in minutes
- Collect singer takes by voice part
- Keep rehearsal prep in one place

CTA:

`Start your first choir pilot`

## Demo Narrative

Use this in every demo:

1. Upload the score your choir already uses.
2. Generate practice audio for rehearsal prep.
3. Ask singers to send section takes back through the same studio.

Keep demos under one minute.

## 30-Day Launch Plan

### Week 1

- polish the landing page
- prepare a 45 to 60 second demo
- create one SATB sample workflow
- define the founding-pilot offer

### Week 2

- reach out to 10 pilot prospects
- offer free setup for the first three songs
- track objections and feedback

### Week 3

- onboard 3 to 5 pilot choirs
- watch how they actually use the workflow
- ask for short written feedback

### Week 4

- turn the best result into a case study
- add one testimonial to the site
- refine pricing from pilot behavior
- start direct outreach with the improved pitch

## Messaging Guardrails

- lead with time saved and faster rehearsal prep
- say "score to rehearsal to singer submissions"
- speak to directors, not generic users
- treat "AI" as secondary language, not the headline
